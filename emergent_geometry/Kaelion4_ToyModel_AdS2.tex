\begin{abstract}
We present a minimal toy model demonstrating that an AdS$_2$ geometry emerges as an output
of an information-theoretic renormalization flow. No gravitational or spacetime assumptions
are made. Geometry arises solely from the dynamics of information accessibility.
\end{abstract}

\section{Toy Model}
We consider a scrambling quantum system and define the information accessibility parameter
$\lambda(t)$ as the average fraction of operator support accessible to a local observer.

\section{RG Flow}
Empirically and analytically, $\lambda$ obeys
\begin{equation}
\frac{d\lambda}{d\ln t} = -c\,\lambda(1-\lambda).
\end{equation}
The solution is
\begin{equation}
\lambda(t) = \frac{1}{1+(t/t_*)^c}.
\end{equation}

\section{Emergent Geometry}
Defining the emergent coordinate $z=-k\ln\lambda$, the RG flow becomes a translation in $z$.
Requiring RG invariance of the effective metric uniquely yields
\begin{equation}
ds^2 = \frac{L^2}{u^2}(du^2 + dx^2),
\end{equation}
which is Euclidean AdS$_2$.

\section{Corollary: Kaelion v3.0 Limit}
At RG fixed points, we recover the entropy correction
\begin{equation}
\alpha = -\frac{1}{2}-\lambda,
\end{equation}
reproducing Kaelion v3.0 as a limiting theory.
